In the future, will East Boston still be a livable neighborhood?

Qingyue Li

What makes a neighborhood livable? In her groundbreaking book, "The Death and Life of Great American Cities," Jane Jacobs argued that successful urban spaces share human scale, walkability, and a mixture of housing, shops, green space, and public buildings and also have mixed building density.

Does East Boston fit this idea of a livable community? And what effect will the changes that are coming to this gentifying neighborhood have on livability?

The Imagine Boston 2030 project asked residents, "What would you add or change to make your neighborhood an even better place to live?"

How well does East Boston compare to people's expectations for a livable neighborhood?
